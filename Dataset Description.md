The dataset used in this study consists of two complementary educational resources: instructional video content and an official curriculum reference. Together, these resources provide both the educational material to be analyzed and the ground-truth curriculum information required for evaluation.

The primary dataset comprises 74 educational videos collected from the official Palestinian Educational Portal (PsELearn) YouTube channel. The selected videos specifically cover Mathematics lessons from the Palestinian third-grade curriculum and represent authentic instructional content developed in accordance with the educational standards of the Palestinian Ministry of Education. The videos include teacher explanations, mathematical demonstrations, worked examples, problem-solving activities, and concept-oriented instructional discussions delivered in Arabic. Since the PsELearn channel contains educational materials for multiple subjects and grade levels, a filtering process was applied to select only the videos relevant to third-grade Mathematics.

To support curriculum-aware analysis and evaluation, the official Palestinian Mathematics Teacher Guide for Grade Three was used as the reference curriculum source. The teacher guide provides structured educational information for each lesson, including learning objectives, targeted skills, prerequisite knowledge, common misconceptions, teaching strategies, assessment methods, and lesson implementation guidelines. Relevant lesson pages were identified and analyzed to construct a structured curriculum knowledge base that serves as the official benchmark for evaluating instructional content.

The collected videos were processed through several stages before analysis. First, the videos were downloaded and converted into audio files. The audio content was then transcribed using the Whisper Large-v3 Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) model, producing Arabic textual transcripts. Since raw ASR outputs may contain linguistic inconsistencies and transcription artifacts, a transcript refinement stage was subsequently applied using the Gemini 2.5 Flash Large Language Model to improve readability, grammatical correctness, and educational clarity while preserving the original instructional meaning. The resulting corrected transcripts formed the primary textual dataset used for educational skill extraction.

Consequently, the final experimental dataset consists of three interconnected components: the educational video transcripts generated from the collected instructional videos, the corrected Arabic transcripts produced through LLM-based refinement, and the structured curriculum knowledge extracted from the Palestinian Mathematics Teacher Guide. This integrated dataset enables the proposed framework to automatically extract educational skills from instructional videos and evaluate their alignment with the official Palestinian curriculum.
